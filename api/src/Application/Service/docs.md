🌳 CO₂ absorbed per year

We estimate how much CO₂ a tree absorbs each year based on its size and growth.

We use the tree’s trunk diameter (and sometimes height) to estimate how much wood it contains.
Trees store carbon as they grow, so we estimate how much the tree grows each year.
That growth is converted into an amount of CO₂ removed from the atmosphere.

👉 In simple terms:
Bigger, faster-growing trees absorb more CO₂ each year.





💧 Water used per year

We estimate how much water a tree uses based on its canopy size and local weather.

The tree’s canopy (leaf area) tells us how much surface is losing water.
We use local weather data (sun, temperature, etc.) to estimate how much water would evaporate.
This gives an estimate of how much water the tree draws from the ground and releases into the air.

👉 In simple terms:
Larger trees in warmer, sunnier areas use more water.
